Coverage Checklist
Five Things Your Policy Must Do
1️⃣
Cover your revenue when a vendor goes down — not just your own systems.
Contingent Business Interruption. This is your Change Healthcare protection. Most policies don't include it — or bury it under a sub-limit. Ask your broker to confirm it explicitly.
2️⃣
Fund your HIPAA defense — even if you did nothing wrong.
OCR investigations are expensive to defend. Panel attorneys must have real HIPAA experience.
3️⃣
Apply full limits to ransomware — no sub-limits. Review your war exclusion.
Some policies cap ransomware coverage well below your total limit. Confirm full limits apply.
4️⃣
Cover website privacy liability — pixels, trackers, patient portal tools.
Carriers are excluding this. Don't assume it's included. It requires explicit confirmation.
5️⃣
Match your limits to your real exposure — not a number from 3 years ago.
Your revenue and patient count have changed. Your limit should reflect that.

Reducing Your Cost of Risk
Four Controls That Lower Both Your Risk and Your Premium
Good security posture = 15–25% lower premium. Insurers now price on controls, not just size.
Highest Impact · Do First
Multi-Factor Authentication — on everything.
Not just leadership email. Every account. Every remote login. Many health centers are halfway there. Finish the job.
Without it:15–30% premium surcharge or coverage declined.
Ransomware Defense
Tested Backups + Endpoint Detection (EDR)
Backups you've actually proven you can restore from. EDR software watching all devices in real time.
Backups are the insurance policy for your insurance policy.
24/7 Monitoring
Security Operations Center (SOC)
Watching your network around the clock. Cuts the 89-day attacker dwell time down to days.
Security cameras on every door — with a guard watching live at 2am Sunday.
The Human Layer
Security Awareness Training — Quarterly
Over half of breaches start with a person being tricked. Training + simulated phishing = your most cost-effective control.
$15K–$40K/yr in these four controls protects against a $2.5M+ incident.

The Tabletop Exercise
"It's 7am Monday. Your EHR is encrypted. What do you do?"
A 2-hour facilitated simulation with your full leadership team. Most health centers have a response plan. Far fewer have tested it with the CEO and CFO in the room — which is exactly where the hard calls get made.

Ask your insurer. Tabletop facilitation is often a free policy benefit.

Action Plan
Three Things to Do Before Your Next Board Meeting
1
Get a Security Risk Assessment done.
Know exactly where your gaps are. Often free through your broker or insurer. Creates evidence of HIPAA due diligence.
2
Confirm MFA is on every account — not just leadership.
Ask your IT team today: "Is MFA enabled on 100% of accounts and remote access points?" If not — this is the week to fix it.
3
Benchmark your current policy — especially for CBI coverage.
Ask your broker: "Are we covered if our billing company or clearinghouse gets hit?" Confirm your limits, your war exclusion, and your privacy liability coverage. Market is favorable right now — don't wait for renewal.
